The Beginning of the End

Overview:

DVDs Enter the Scene (late '90s)

When DVDs came out in the late '90s, they were a massive upgrade — better picture quality, no rewinding, bonus features, and they didn’t degrade over time like tapes. DVDs were also cheaper to produce and distribute.

Digital Formats & Streaming (2000s+)

Once the internet got fast enough, people began downloading or streaming movies. Services like Netflix (originally a DVD-by-mail service) began to shift toward streaming, making physical media less relevant.

Convenience & Quality

VHS tapes were bulky, prone to wear and tear, and required VCRs (which were mechanical and finicky). DVDs and then streaming offered instant access, better resolution, and less hassle.
